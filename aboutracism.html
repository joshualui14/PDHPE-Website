What is Racism?

Racism is a form of discrimination against a certain person or group of people that belong to a specific ethinc or racial group. Racism can appear in many forms through spoken words and actions such as racist name-calling and exclusion from certain activities. Racism can have a detrimental effect to the mental state of both individuals and communities.

Extent of Racism within Australia

Racism is a common issue that is experienced within the majority of people within Australia.

  • 7 in 10 children experienced racism within their childhood, most commonly within school
  • 1 in 2 Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australians experience racism at sporting events
  • 1 in 3 people experience racism within the workplace
  • Only 1 in 3 people stand up against racism
  • 1 in 5 people that are living in Australia have received racism over the past year. This increased from 1 in 8 in the previous year
  • 2 in 5 people were targets of verbal racial abuse
  • Half of all Australian residents from culturally diverse background have experienced discrimination in their lifetime
  • 7 in 10 teenagers have experienced racism

Effect on Individuals

Racism can have lasting mental health impacts on many young people including:

  • Constant feelings of sadness, anger, anxiety and depression
  • Fear of physical or verbal abuse
  • Having a lack of trust in others
  • Headaches and trembling
  • Lower self esteem
  • Reduced want to attend school and social activities

These effects can cause a person to have low aspects of their mental health and a reduced general wellbeing causing them to fall into a lifeless state.

Effects on Communities

Racism can have a major impact on communities within Australia due to the multicultural factor that affects numerous people.

  • Lessens the trust and respect among people in society
  • Creates psychological distress
  • Worries for others in their community that they will also receive racism
  • Loss of cultural identity
  • Causes moral tensions
  • Causes unstable and potentially dangerous communities
